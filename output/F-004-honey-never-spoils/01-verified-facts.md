# F-004 — Verified Facts: Honey never spoils

**Method:** Targeted cite-check (not the full deep-research fan-out — this is a well-established
empirical fact with one embellishment trap; see the research-tier note below). Cite-or-abstain:
every claim the script uses must trace to a verbatim quote + URL here. Primary source = Smithsonian
Magazine (Natasha Geiling, 2013), the canonical authoritative write-up that covers BOTH the chemistry
and the archaeological preservation.

## What to AVOID (the embellishment trap — abstain)
- **Do NOT state "3,000-year-old honey from King Tut's / Tutankhamun's tomb was still edible."** This
  is repeated all over the viral-facts internet (History Facts, beekeeping blogs, etc.) but **no
  authoritative source confirms the "still edible from a specific 3,000-yr Egyptian/King-Tut find"
  framing.** Smithsonian — the best source — says only "thousands of years old… still preserved" with
  **no specific age and no King Tut.** Use C6's "thousands of years old, still preserved" framing, or
  the genuinely-sourced ~5,500-yr Georgia record (C7), not the King Tut specific.
- Do not claim honey *literally* never spoils with no conditions — the honest claim is **sealed, pure
  honey** (C4). "Crystallized = spoiled" is also false (C5).

## Sparse-facts gate (structural)
Intended ~4-beat structure, ≥1 distinct high/medium claim per beat, ≥5 total — **PASS** (6 HIGH + 1 MEDIUM):
- **Beat 1 (hook):** everything in your kitchen has an expiry date — except one food → premise framed off C6 (the preserved-for-millennia payoff).
- **Beat 2 (why / chemistry):** C1 (low moisture) + C2 (acidity) + C3 (hydrogen peroxide) — three independent mechanisms.
- **Beat 3 (reveal / accent ✓):** C6 (Egyptian-tomb honey, thousands of years old, still preserved) [optional sharper: C7, ~5,500 yrs].
- **Beat 4 (caveat / loop):** C4 (only sealed honey) + C5 (crystallizing ≠ spoiling).
