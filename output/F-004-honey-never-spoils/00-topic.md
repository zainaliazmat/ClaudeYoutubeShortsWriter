## Handoff to research (step 2)
- **Research question:** "Is it true that honey effectively never spoils, and that archaeologists found edible ~3,000-year-old honey in an ancient Egyptian tomb? What is the precise age/provenance of the oldest known still-preserved honey, and what chemistry (water activity, pH, hydrogen peroxide) makes honey non-perishable when sealed?"
- **Must verify (the claims the script hinges on):**
  1. The oldest known honey found still preserved/edible — its **age** (~3,000 years? older?) and **provenance** (Egyptian tomb / which find) — verbatim quote + authoritative source (e.g. National Geographic, Smithsonian, a food-science body).
  2. The **mechanism**: low water activity (hygroscopic, ~17% water), acidic pH (~3.9), and hydrogen peroxide from the glucose-oxidase enzyme — why microbes can't grow.
  3. The **caveat/boundary** the script must respect: this holds for **sealed, pure, properly stored** honey; honey can crystallize (still edible) and can ferment if it absorbs moisture — so frame as "never spoils when sealed," not an absolute with no conditions.
  4. The single payoff number/line for the accent beat (the "still edible after N years ✓" verdict) traceable to a quote + URL.
