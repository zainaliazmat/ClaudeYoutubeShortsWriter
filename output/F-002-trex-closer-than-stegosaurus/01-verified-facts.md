## Derived (scale-honest, computed FROM the cited values)
- **Stegosaurus → T. rex gap** = 150 − 66 = **~84 million years** (using Stego ~150 Mya, T. rex ~66 Mya).
- **T. rex → today gap** = **~66 million years** (T. rex extinction, end-Cretaceous).
- **Therefore:** 66 My (T. rex→now) < 84 My (Stego→T. rex). T. rex is **~18 million years closer**
  to the present than to Stegosaurus. Both gaps computed only from the two cited ranges — the
  data-viz segment lengths must be proportional to 84 : 66 (= 1.27).

## Abstentions / guardrails
- Use round anchors (~150 Mya, ~66 Mya) — the sources give ranges; do not assert false precision.
- "Closer to you than to Stegosaurus" is the safe framing (gap-since < gap-before); avoid implying
  T. rex and humans coexisted.
