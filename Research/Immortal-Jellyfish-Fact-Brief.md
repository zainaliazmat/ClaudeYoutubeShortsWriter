# Fact Brief — The Immortal Jellyfish (*Turritopsis dohrnii*)

> Fact-checked research brief for a Fathom ~7-min video. Every claim below was verified by multi-source, adversarial (3-vote) checking. **Use only these locked facts in the script.** Refuted/uncertain items are flagged at the bottom — do NOT use them.

---

## One-paragraph summary (the honest version)

*Turritopsis dohrnii* achieves **"biological immortality"** by reverting from its sexually mature adult (medusa) stage back to a juvenile **polyp** through a process called **transdifferentiation**. When stressed, damaged, starved, hit by temperature/salinity shifts, or simply old, the medusa shrinks, settles onto a surface, and over **~24–72 hours** transforms via a chitin-protected **cyst** stage into a polyp — restarting its life cycle, theoretically forever. **Crucially: "biologically immortal" means no built-in aging limit — NOT "cannot die."** Individuals are routinely eaten, get diseased, or die from other hazards, and most wild ones die as medusae without ever reverting. It is the only known animal that can **repeatedly** rejuvenate *after* its adults have sexually reproduced.

## LOCKED FACTS (verified — safe to use)

### 1. The immortality mechanism
- A stressed, damaged, senescent, or starved **medusa** (adult) shrinks, settles on a surface, and transforms through a **cyst stage** back into a **juvenile polyp** in **~24–72 hours**, via **transdifferentiation**. It then grows **stolons first, then new polyps**, forming a new clonal colony — theoretically repeating indefinitely. *(high confidence, 3-0)*
- **Transdifferentiation** = a mature, specialized cell transforms directly into a *different* type of mature cell. (This is the headline "magic trick.")
- The cyst is a "ball-like stage" of poorly differentiated cells inside a protective chitinous shell.
- ⚠️ Caveat for honesty: no single-cell lineage tracking has confirmed transdifferentiation at the individual-cell level yet — the term reflects histological (tissue-level) observation. (Don't need to belabor on-screen, but don't overclaim cellular proof.)

### 2. What triggers the reset
- Triggered **two ways**: (a) sub-lethal **environmental stress** — starvation, temperature change, lower salinity, physical injury; OR (b) an **internal, genetically programmed** trigger when it reaches sexual maturity (the onset of senescence). *(3-0)*
- **All medusa stages** can revert, and reversal can even happen **spontaneously**, with no injury or environmental change. *(3-0)*
- A 2024 lab study quantified reversal (cyst formation) rates by trigger: **heat shock 88%, starvation 80%, wounding 72%, salinity 64%** — and ~22% **failed** to revert. So reversal is a **capacity, not a guarantee.** *(Great honest stat to include.)*

### 3. "Only" immortal animal — with the honest caveat
- *T. dohrnii* is **the only known metazoan (animal) able to repeatedly rejuvenate after its medusae sexually reproduce.** Its relative **_Turritopsis rubra_** is the standard NON-immortal comparison. *(PNAS 2022, 3-0)*
- ⚠️ Soften the absolute "only": other *Turritopsis* species can revert after reproduction, but only *T. dohrnii* keeps near-100% post-reproductive rejuvenation. A **2024 report** found reverse development in a **comb jelly (_Mnemiopsis leidyi_)** after stress — mildly contesting the absolute "only" framing (but that's not repeated post-reproductive rejuvenation). Safe phrasing: *"the only animal known to do this **on repeat, after reproducing**."*

### 4. Immortal ≠ invincible (THE key myth to bust)
- "Biologically immortal" = **no intrinsic aging/senescence limit.** It does NOT mean it can't die. Individuals still die from **predation, disease, and other hazards**, and **most wild medusae die before ever reverting.** Hence scientists' careful term: **"potential immortality."** *(3-0)*

### 5. The genetics (attribute carefully — three SEPARATE studies)
- **2022 PNAS comparative-genomics study** (Pascual-Torner, López-Otín, Quesada — the "Spanish study"): sequenced & compared the whole genomes of **_T. dohrnii_ vs. the non-immortal _T. rubra_**. Found variants/expansions in genes for **DNA repair, telomere maintenance, redox (antioxidant) balance, replication, stem-cell population**, and **silencing of PRC2 targets + activation of pluripotency factors (NANOG/OCT4/SOX2/MYC-type)** during life-cycle reversal. *(3-0)*
- **2021 GBE expression-profiling study:** cyst stage is enriched for aging/lifespan genes, DNA-repair/tumor-suppressor genes (e.g., **BRCA1**), and transposable-element control. *(3-0)*
- **2023 DNA Research study** (Hasegawa et al., Japan): first **long-read genome**, ~**435.9 Mb, 23,314 genes**, profiled across **9 time points (0–51h)**; **23.8–36.1%** of stage-specific genes were lineage-specific **"no-hit" genes** (no known counterpart — i.e., genuinely mysterious). *(3-0)*
- ⚠️ **Do NOT conflate the three.** The 435.9 Mb / 23,314-gene figures = Japanese study, not the "Spanish study." The PRC2/pluripotency/telomere findings = PNAS 2022. The genes are **proposed/putative** drivers — correlated, **not yet functionally proven** to cause rejuvenation.

### 6. How it conquered the world (invasive spread)
- It has spread **worldwide via human shipping**: one mitochondrial 16S genetic clade from **Japan, Pacific & Atlantic Panama, Florida, Spain, and Italy** differs by only **~0.31%** of base-pairs — evidence of recent, human-mediated global spread. *(Miglietta & Lessios 2008, 3-0)*
- Most likely vector: **ballast water of cargo ships (as medusae)** or **ship hulls (as polyps).** Its reversal ability helps it survive long voyages. Described as a **"silent invasion."**
- ⚠️ Naming note: older foundational papers used the synonym **_Turritopsis nutricula_ / _Turritopsis sp._** before the lineage was recognized as *T. dohrnii* (Weismann, 1883).

### 7. Discovery & key researchers
- Reverse development / cell transdifferentiation in *Turritopsis* was **first documented by Piraino, Boero, Aeschbach & Schmid (1996)** in *Biological Bulletin* ("Reversing the Life Cycle…"). **Ferdinando Boero** and **Stefano Piraino** are the foundational figures. *(medium confidence — derived)*
- **Schmich et al. 2007** (Piraino lab) detailed the triggers. Genome era: **Pascual-Torner et al. (Spain, PNAS 2022)** and **Hasegawa et al. (Japan, 2023).**

### 8. Human / anti-aging relevance — keep it SPECULATIVE
- Studying it **may** illuminate human aging, regeneration, and disease (e.g., cancer), with **potential** pharmacological relevance. *(3-0)*
- BUT real human rejuvenation **"remains firmly in the realm of science fiction"** (researchers' own words). Mechanisms may not transfer; in humans, the same cellular reprogramming would risk **cancer** rather than rejuvenation. **Do not imply a fountain of youth is near.**

### 9. Basic profile
- **Size:** tiny — adult medusa roughly **4.5 mm** across (about the size of a pinky fingernail / smaller than). (Commonly cited; keep as "a few millimetres — smaller than your little fingernail.")
- **Lifecycle stages:** fertilized egg → larva (planula) → **polyp colony** → **medusa** (adult) → [under stress/age] → cyst → back to **polyp**.
- **Origin:** native to the Mediterranean; now found in temperate-to-tropical oceans worldwide.

## ⚠️ DO NOT USE (refuted or unverified)

- ❌ **"Shin Kubota sustained a colony that reincarnated itself 11 times in two years."** — REFUTED (vote 1-2). Kubota is a real, widely-cited popularizer/aquarist, but the specific "11 times" figure did not survive verification. **Do not state as fact.**
- ❌ **"Reversal has never been observed in the wild."** — part of the same refuted claim. Do not assert.
- ⚠️ Avoid stating any implicated gene **causes** immortality — they are correlated/proposed only.
- ⚠️ Avoid "it cannot die" / "it lives forever" framing — factually wrong; the whole hook should lean INTO correcting this.

## Open questions (honest unknowns — good for a "what we still don't know" beat)
- Has any single individual been tracked through more than a handful of full cycles? Is "indefinite" empirical or purely theoretical? (Theoretical.)
- What exactly triggers *spontaneous* (non-stress) reversal at maturity?
- Has transdifferentiation been confirmed at the single-cell level? (Not yet.)
- Which implicated genes are functionally validated vs. merely correlated? (None functionally validated yet.)
